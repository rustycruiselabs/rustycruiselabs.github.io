Devlog #8 - Unity Advertisements

Using Mobile Ad Monetization for our games

Overview

As much as we love making games, we still need to sustain ourselves. That’s why monetization is a crucial part of our development process, it allows us to keep creating while ensuring our work remains accessible to players.

Since this is our first game, we decided to go with a freemium model, combining advertisements (Ads) and in-app purchases (IAPs) as our primary monetization strategies. In this devlog, we’ll focus on how we implemented advertisement monetization and the thought process behind our approach.

Note that it is not a step by step guide, but more of a quick reference for myself and the issues I faced while implementing

Our Implementation

Mobile Ads Strategies

We personally dislike invasive advertisements, so we aimed to find a balanced approach that keeps monetization effective without disrupting the player experience. After researching different ad formats, here’s what we decided:

  1. Banner Ads (X - Not Implemented) - We chose not to use banner ads because they take up valuable screen space and can ruin the visual aesthetics of a mobile game.

  2. Rewarded Ads (O - Implemented) - Players prefer having a choice rather than being forced to watch ads. Rewarded ads provide an optional way for players to earn in-game currency, making them our primary ad format.

  3. Interstitial Ads (O) - Inspired by Crossy Road, we decided to show interstitial ads after a certain number of games or a set playtime. This keeps them non-intrusive while ensuring they contribute meaningfully to monetization. Additionally, we can offer an IAP to remove these ads for players who prefer an uninterrupted experience.

Therefore, we will be using Rewarded Ads and Interstitial Ads as our main ad formats, ensuring a good balance between monetization and player experience.

Issues

1) Unity Ads Error loading Ad Unit Rewarded_iOS: INVALID_ARGUMENT - adMarkup is missing; objectId is missing

At some point, I tried to integrate with Unity LevelPlay which changed the settings of my Unity Ads Monetization. This results in my advertisement to not load with the following error “Error loading Ad Unit Rewarded_iOS: INVALID_ARGUMENT - adMarkup is missing; objectId is missing”. As mentioned in Unity forum, this error is usualy due to incorrect mediation partner settings.

To resolve, simply go to Unity Cloud - Unity Ads Monetization and verify your mediation partner settings. If you are only using Unity Ads, ensure that you choose I only use Unity Ads, as shown in screenshot below.
